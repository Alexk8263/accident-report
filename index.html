Accident Report Instructions (Company Drivers)
Use the tabs above to document the accident and send the report to the office.
✅ What to do (in order)
  1. Checklist — make sure everyone is safe, call 911 if needed.
  2. Scene — enter location, time, police info, and what happened.
  3. Vehicles — fill in your vehicle + the other vehicle(s).
  4. Photos — take pictures of damage, plates, and the full scene.
  5. QR Code — if needed, share this form quickly with a witness/driver.
  6. Send — review the summary and submit to the office.
📌 Tips for drivers
  • Don’t admit fault or argue.
  • Get witness contact info if possible.
  • Take wide + close-up photos (multiple angles).
  • If police respond, record the report # and officer name.

📋 Photo Checklist
  • My vehicle — front, rear, both sides
  • Other vehicle(s) — all sides
  • Both license plates
  • Close-up of all damage
  • Wide shot of full accident scene
  • Skid marks / road debris
  • Traffic signs / signals nearby
  • Insurance / ID cards of other driver
  • Any visible injuries (with consent)
🚨 Immediate Actions
  • Check everyone for injuries — call 911 if needed
  • Move vehicles out of traffic if safe to do so
  • Turn on hazard lights
  • Set out road flares or triangles if available
  • Call police (even for minor accidents)
  • Do NOT admit fault or apologize
📋 Information to Collect
  • Other driver's name, address, phone
  • Other driver's license number & state
  • Other vehicle's plate, make, model, color
  • Other driver's insurance company & policy #
  • Names & contact info of all witnesses
  • Police report number & officer info
📞 After the Scene
  • Call your insurance claims line
  • Send photos and report to your insurer
  • Keep copy of police report
  • See a doctor even if you feel fine
  • Do NOT post about accident on social media
  • Keep records of all accident-related expenses
